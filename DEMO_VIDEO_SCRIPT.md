# NeighborNet — Demo Video Script (~3 min)

Record screen (phone or laptop browser) + your voice. Keep energy up,
speak like you're showing a friend, not reading a script.

---

## 1. Problem (0:00–0:15)
**Say:**
"Millions of people in cities like Delhi live near a free clinic, a food
bank, or a shelter — and never know it exists. There's no single place
to find essential services near you, especially if you don't have
someone to ask. NeighborNet fixes that."

**Show:** App homepage, map loaded with pins visible.

## 2. Core walkthrough (0:15–2:15)

**Near me (0:15–0:35)**
- Click "Near me". Allow location.
- **Say:** "One tap centers the map on you and sorts everything by
  distance — no typing an address."

**Category filter (0:35–1:00)**
- Click "Health" only, then add "Food".
- **Say:** "You can filter by exactly what you need — health, food,
  water, shelter, or education — so you're not scrolling through
  everything."

**Search (1:00–1:15)**
- Type a neighborhood name in search.
- **Say:** "Or just search by name or area."

**Map → List toggle + card detail (1:15–1:40)**
- Toggle to List view.
- Click "Get directions" on a card (show it opens Google Maps).
- **Say:** "Every listing has hours, contact info, and one-tap
  directions."

**Add a resource — the core loop (1:40–2:15)**
- Click "+ Add resource".
- Click a spot on the mini-map, fill name/category/address.
- Submit.
- Switch back to main map — **show the new pin appears live, no refresh.**
- **Say:** "And this is the important part — anyone can add a resource
  in under a minute. No login, no approval queue. The map gets better
  every time someone contributes. It's crowdsourced access."

## 3. Tech + impact framing (2:15–2:45)
**Say:**
"NeighborNet is built with React, TypeScript, and Firebase, with
OpenStreetMap for the map — so it's free to run and easy to scale.
Right now it's seeded with real essential services in Delhi — Mohalla
Clinics, DUSIB night shelters, public libraries, blood banks — all
pulled from public records, not made up. The goal is simple: access to
essential services shouldn't depend on who you happen to know."

## 4. Future work (2:45–3:00)
**Say:**
"Next steps: offline support for low-connectivity areas, multi-language
support, and a lightweight verification layer so trusted contributors
can confirm listings stay accurate. Thanks for watching."

---

## Recording tips
- Do a dry run once before recording — know exactly which
  resource you'll click "Add" for, so the demo doesn't stall.
- Keep your test "Add resource" entry realistic (not "test123") in case
  it's visible in the recording.
- Record at 1920x1080 if possible; Devpost embeds YouTube/Vimeo links.
- If you fumble a line, keep going — better than 6 takes of perfection
  eating your remaining hours.
